One of the main reasons I want to visit Venus is its fascinating resemblance to Earth. Although Venus is often described as Earth's twin, it is quite different in reality. While Earth has a diverse range of environments, such as mountains, forests, and oceans, Venus is covered by a thick atmosphere mainly composed of carbon dioxide, making it extremely hot and inhospitable. The average temperature on Venus is a scorching 900 degrees Fahrenheit (475 degrees Celsius), which is hotter than the surface of Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun!

Imagine being on a planet where everything is covered by a dense layer of clouds!
